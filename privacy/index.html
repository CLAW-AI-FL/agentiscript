1. Overview

We collect minimal data. We do not sell your personal information. Ever. AgentiScript operates as a lean, privacy-respecting service. This policy explains what data we collect, why, how long we keep it, and your rights regarding it — including the unique privacy implications of blockchain-based transactions.

This Privacy Policy applies to agentiscript.com (the "Site"), the x402 API, the AgentiScript npm package, and all associated services operated by AgentiScript (collectively, the "Service"). It describes how we handle personal information in accordance with applicable privacy law, including the Florida Information Protection Act (FIPA) and, to the extent applicable, the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA).

By using the Service, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, please discontinue use of the Service.

2. Information We Collect

We collect only the information necessary to operate the Service effectively and securely. The categories of data we may collect include:

2.1 Automatically Collected Data (Server & Analytics)

  • IP addresses — collected in server access logs for security, abuse prevention, and rate limiting. IP addresses may be truncated or anonymized where technically feasible.
  • Browser type and version, operating system, device type — collected via analytics to understand how the Service is used and to optimize compatibility.
  • Referring URLs and page navigation paths — to understand traffic sources and user flow.
  • Time and date of access, pages visited, time spent on pages — for performance monitoring and improvement.
  • Error logs — to identify and resolve technical issues.

2.2 Information You Voluntarily Provide

  • GitHub username or profile — if you contact us via GitHub Issues, we receive your GitHub username, any identifying information in your profile, and the content of your communications.
  • Email address — only if you voluntarily provide it in support correspondence. We do not run a mailing list or newsletter without your opt-in.
  • Purchase information — if you purchase a Pro Pack, payment is processed by our third-party payment processor. We receive confirmation of purchase and may receive a transaction reference ID, but we do not store full credit card numbers or payment credentials.

2.3 API Usage Data

  • API request logs — endpoint called, timestamp, response code, and approximate geographic region (country-level).
  • Wallet addresses — if you use the x402 API or purchase NFTs, your XRPL wallet address is associated with your transactions. See Section 3 for important details about blockchain data.
  • API access patterns — to enforce rate limits, detect abuse, and improve service performance.

We do not collect: Social Security numbers, government-issued ID numbers, health or medical information, biometric data, financial account numbers (beyond what our payment processor handles independently), or sensitive personal information beyond what is described above.

3. Blockchain Data & XRPL Transparency

⛓ Critical Notice — Blockchain Immutability: Transactions conducted on the XRP Ledger (XRPL) are recorded on a public, decentralized, immutable blockchain. This means that certain data associated with your NFT purchases and x402 API payments is permanently visible to anyone in the world and cannot be deleted, modified, or "forgotten" — by us or by you. This is a fundamental characteristic of public blockchain technology, not a policy choice by AgentiScript.

3.1 What Is Publicly Visible on the XRPL

The following information is written to the XRP Ledger as part of normal XRPL operation and is permanently, publicly accessible to anyone with access to the internet via XRPL explorers (such as xrpl.org, XRPScan, Bithomp, and others):

  • Your XRPL wallet address — a pseudonymous public key, but one that may be linked to your real identity if you have used that wallet in association with a centralized exchange or other identifying service.
  • Transaction amounts — the XRP or token amount transferred in each transaction.
  • Transaction timestamps — the exact ledger close time of each transaction.
  • NFT token IDs and metadata — including the NFT URI, which may contain descriptive metadata about the icon.
  • Transaction memos (if any) — any memo fields attached to your transactions are publicly visible.
  • Complete transaction history — all historical transactions associated with your wallet address are permanently accessible.

3.2 Pseudonymity vs. Anonymity

Your XRPL wallet address provides pseudonymity, not anonymity. If your wallet address is publicly linked to your real identity (e.g., through a centralized exchange that has performed KYC/AML verification), then all transactions associated with that wallet — including AgentiScript NFT purchases and API payments — may be traceable to you. AgentiScript has no control over this linkage and cannot prevent it.

3.3 On-Chain Data Is Beyond Our Control

Because blockchain data is decentralized and immutable, AgentiScript cannot delete, modify, or restrict access to any data recorded on the XRP Ledger. If you exercise any "right to erasure" or "right to be forgotten" under applicable privacy law, we can remove data from our own systems, but we cannot remove data from the public XRPL blockchain. By conducting transactions on the XRPL, you acknowledge and accept this irreversibility.

3.4 x402 API Payment Data

When you use the x402 micropayment API, each API call that triggers a payment creates an on-chain XRPL transaction. This transaction is immediately and permanently public. We may also retain internal logs of API call metadata (endpoint, timestamp, response code) for up to 90 days for abuse prevention and billing reconciliation purposes.

3.5 Third-Party Blockchain Explorers

AgentiScript has no relationship with, control over, or responsibility for any third-party XRPL blockchain explorers, analytics services, or data aggregators that index and display public blockchain data. Their privacy policies and data practices are independent of ours.

4. How We Use Your Information

We use collected information solely for the following purposes:

  • Service operation — to provide, maintain, and improve the AgentiScript website, icon catalog, x402 API, and associated services.
  • Security and abuse prevention — to detect, prevent, and respond to fraud, unauthorized access, scraping, IP theft, rate limit violations, and other prohibited conduct.
  • Analytics and optimization — to understand how the Service is used so we can improve it. Analytics data is processed in aggregate and pseudonymized where possible.
  • Transaction processing and license management — to process Pro Pack purchases, issue licenses, and reconcile API payments.
  • Legal compliance — to comply with applicable laws, court orders, legal process, and regulatory requirements.
  • IP enforcement — to identify and take action against unauthorized uses of AgentiScript intellectual property, including unauthorized scraping, AI training on icons, and trademark misuse.
  • Support and communications — to respond to inquiries and support requests you submit via GitHub Issues or other channels.

We do not use your data for: targeted advertising, sale to data brokers, profiling for marketing, or any purpose beyond those listed above.

5. Cookies & Analytics

Analytics: We use Google Analytics 4 (GA4) with anonymized IP addresses to collect aggregate usage statistics. GA4 data is processed by Google LLC under their privacy policy. We use this data in aggregate only to understand overall traffic patterns and page performance. You can opt out of Google Analytics tracking using the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

Essential Cookies: We may use minimal essential cookies necessary for the Service to function (e.g., session management, CSRF protection). These cannot be opted out of while using the Service.

No Advertising Cookies: We do not use advertising cookies, tracking pixels, or any third-party behavioral advertising technology.

Our website does not currently display a cookie consent banner, as we limit our use to essential operational cookies and anonymized analytics. Users in jurisdictions requiring explicit consent (e.g., EU/EEA under ePrivacy Directive) should be aware that our analytics use anonymized data and is structured to minimize consent requirements.

7. Data Retention

We retain personal data only as long as necessary for the purposes described in this policy, or as required by law. The following table summarizes our retention practices:

Data Type Retention Period Basis
Server access logs (IP, timestamp, endpoint) 90 days Security, abuse prevention
Aggregated analytics data (GA4) 14 months (GA4 default, then aggregated indefinitely) Service improvement
x402 API call logs (wallet address, endpoint, timestamp) 90 days Billing reconciliation, abuse prevention
Pro Pack purchase records (transaction ID, license scope) 7 years Legal/tax compliance

Upon expiration of the applicable retention period, we will securely delete or anonymize personal data in our systems (excluding blockchain data, which we cannot control). Legal requirements may require us to retain certain data for longer periods.

8. Data Security

We implement reasonable and appropriate technical and organizational security measures to protect your personal information against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. These measures include:

  • HTTPS/TLS encryption for all data transmitted between your browser and our servers.
  • Access controls limiting internal access to personal data to those with a legitimate need.
  • Regular review of our data collection practices to minimize the scope of data retained.
  • Prompt investigation of security incidents and notification to affected users as required by applicable law.

However, no method of internet transmission or electronic storage is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security. In the event of a data breach affecting your personal information, we will notify affected parties as required by the Florida Information Protection Act (FIPA), Section 501.171, Florida Statutes, and other applicable law.

Note regarding blockchain security: XRPL transactions are secured by the decentralized consensus mechanism of the XRP Ledger. The security of your XRPL wallet is your sole responsibility. Never share your wallet seed phrase or private key with anyone, including AgentiScript. We will never ask for your private key.

9. Your Privacy Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have certain rights regarding your personal information. We respect these rights and will respond to valid requests within the timeframes required by applicable law.

Rights Available Under Applicable Law (may include):

  • Right to Access — the right to know what personal data we hold about you and to receive a copy.
  • Right to Correction — the right to correct inaccurate personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to Deletion / Erasure — the right to request deletion of your personal data from our systems. Important limitation: we cannot delete data recorded on the public XRPL blockchain. See Section 3.
  • Right to Data Portability — where applicable, the right to receive your personal data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Right to Object / Opt-Out — the right to object to certain processing, including analytics. See Section 5 for analytics opt-out.
  • Right to Restrict Processing — the right to request that we restrict processing of your data in certain circumstances.
  • California Residents (CCPA): You have the right to know, right to delete, right to opt-out of sale (we do not sell personal information), and right to non-discrimination for exercising these rights.

To exercise your rights, please open an issue at: github.com/CLAW-AI-FL/agentiscript/issues and include sufficient information for us to identify your data and verify your identity. We may request additional verification to process your request.

We will respond to valid requests within 30 days, or within the period required by applicable law. If we cannot fulfill a request (e.g., due to a legal obligation to retain data, or blockchain immutability), we will explain why.
